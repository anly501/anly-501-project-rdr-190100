Word Cloud

Word clouds (also known as text clouds or tag clouds) work in a simple way: the more a specific word appears in a source of textual data (such as a speech, blog post, or database), the bigger and bolder it appears in the word cloud. A word cloud is a collection, or cluster, of words depicted in different sizes. The bigger and bolder the word appears, the more often itâ€™s mentioned within a given text and the more important it is. I have created 10 wordclouds, 1 for each team.

Scatter Plot - Polarity vs Subjectivity

In sentiment analysis, polarity value lies between [-1,1] and is used to determine the Sentiment of any text. Polarity closer to -1 indicates negative sentiment while closer to 1 indicates a positive sentiment. Subjectivity on the other hand has values between [0,1] and is defined as the degree of personal emotion and passion in any text. This graph is particularly interesting since some tweets even after having a polarity lower than 0 are classified as Positive. One reason for this can be that even though polarity is negative, they have a very high subjectivity which means their tweet is passionate and full of personal emotions.

Bar Plot - Comparison of Team vs Polarity and Subjectivity

When you plot the Average of Polarity and Subjectivity against Teams, there's one interesting thing to be noticed. There are a lot of negative sentiment tweets regarding Ferrari, the people are passionate while tweeting about the team and have a personal touch while tweeting about the team and the same goes for other teams as well such as Redbull, Alpine and Aston Martin.
