In today’s world, data exists everywhere. It applies to transportation, healthcare, supply chain, social media, e-mails, the stock market, academia and almost everything. Statistics show that 500+ terabytes of data are generated by Facebook every day. During my academic years and with the industrial exposure so far, I have realized that Data Science, at its foundation, is the study of extracting vital information from structured and unstructured data. This area of study also concentrates hugely on being able to derive informed decisions and tactical moves from available data often termed as ‘insights’. The insights thus gained have significant advantages and this make Statistics an important part of Data Science, as they stand as a fundamental part of the approach. In the present era, Data Science and Analytics are growing at a fast rate and organizations require professionals who may scrutinize data and help drive instant business decisions competently. I studied Data Science at the Undergraduate Level too at NMIMS University which helped me gain a lot of theoretical knowledge towards the concepts involved in Data Science.
My undergraduate course captivated me to further delve into and advance my career in this massive and interesting field. Sharing briefly, I was intrigued by the power of data and the features present in the data that help give out meaningful insights and predictions learned in courses like Machine Learning and Computer Vision. Also, Natural Language Processing helped me gain perspective on how a simple line of text can contain so many different attributes that can be modified and trained as per our needs. Advanced Neural Networks helped me learn how some features that might be neglected during machine learning and how different activation functions and hidden layers might help us focus on that. Statistical Methods helped me understand insightful tests such p test, chi-squared test using advanced Excel. Determined to upskill myself, I also took up certifications in Complete Data Science Bootcamp, Python for Data Science and Machine Learning Bootcamp, Natural Language Processing with Python, Python for Computer Vision with OpenCV and Deep Learning, Tableau A–Z, Deep Learning A–Z, TensorFlow for Deep Learning with Python, SQL for Data Analytics and Business Intelligence, Microsoft Power BI for Business Intelligence, Business Intelligence Analyst. As these topics are directly related to the intended Master’s program, they have helped me build a strong foundation for the same.
Furthering my interest in Computer Vision and Deep Learning has been my final year project on Football Penalty Direction Predictor Using Pose Estimation and Deep Learning. This mainly focused on the concept of penalties in football that are usually very unpredictable. Approximately 95% of the penalties turn out to be successful. The pose of the penalty shooter before he/she shoots the ball is considered to predict the direction where the ball would go to help make a shot less unpredictable. For this, I manually crafted a custom dataset of over 250 videos containing more than 2500 penalty shoot- outs and used these videos and images to get the predictions. During this, I came across constraints such as poor data quality and also there was a lot of brute force work such as manually cropping the images and labeling them. I could overcome this by acquiring necessary knowledge on designing, model building, predictions, and calculation of accuracy metrics. The other projects that I undertook included Facial Attribute Detection, Emotion Detection from Speech, Finding Donors Classification, Sentiment Analyzer, and Housing Data Analysis. I could develop an affinity for challenges through these projects and they have enabled me to think out of the box, which in the long run would help me with real-time problems and finding solutions to them would come naturally.

Through these, I could acquire hands-on experience in data extraction, cleaning, and pre-processing that are essential for data modelling. My training helped me with technical learning and the skills learned are very likely to boost my career.
